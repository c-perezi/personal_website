# How My View of AI as "Intelligence" Changed

Before this course, I found it hard to concretely define intelligence, and one of the first forums precisely asked us to do so. I could say that intelligence was a multi-dimensional skill, including but not limited to academic, emotional, social, and even physical domains. What was definitely true is that I had not yet come up with a concensus on the topic. 

The question of intelligence after working hands-on with LLMs and most importantly analyzing them has not yet been answered has provoked deep reflection. On one hand, there is _something_ clearly "intelligent" about LLMs. They are able to reason, and so long as they have been trained correctly, provide generally good results - much like us humans. 

However, there's something uniquely human about intelligence - something that in our "Is AI True Intelligence?" essay - and creativity that undermines my ability to fully consider AI as true intelligence, and it is the concept of _qualia_. Human intelligence is rooted in perception, and artificial intelligence is not able to perceive unless it is either prompted to, trained, or allowed to do so. This is the main point I take issue yet, and unfortunately have not yet fully deliberated on.
