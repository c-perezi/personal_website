# Reflection on New Course Experiences 

## Midterm Exam 

I tend to struggle a lot with exams, especially because I find it hard to connect the course preparation material and the questions asked in evaluations. I was quite surprised that was not the case in ICOM 5015's midterm exam. Though challenging, I appreciated the structure and confidence-based grading, and the analytical problems on LLMs and hybrid systems let me express my throught process cleanly. Overall it was a good experience. 

## Essay and Debate: Is AI real intelligence that is artificial? 

The essay and debate regarding "Is AI real intelligence that is artificial?" was far more philosophical than I envisioned when I signed up for an ICOM course - but not in a bad way. I think that it was a good change of pace in something I believe is ever more crucial in engineering: the ability to reason, disagree, and most importantly express our points. 

## Overall learning experience in ICOM5015

This was my first and likely only ICOM course, taken as an elective for my Master's of Science in Industrial Engineering. 

### What I liked 

- **Forums**. Being able to research and discuss a topic was fairly instructive because it prompted me to think about what I had to write. 
- **Midterm structure**. In part this is discussed above, but I'd like to reiterate it here - I truly felt that the midterm tested my knowledge fairly. 
- **Programming assignments**. The programming assignments were challenging, but interesting. 

### What could be better 

- **Group dynamics**. Scheduling and submitting your best work is sometimes difficult in an in-person group setting, but an online setting adds a layer of complexity. 
- **Course notifications**. Consistent course notifications - or a more predictable cadence in quizzes, forums, and programming assignments - could go a long way in making sure no evaluations are missed. 

Overall, I'd take a continuation of the course without a doubt - it is challenging, relevant to my work, and overall fun to study.
